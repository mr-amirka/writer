## Overview

A stone farmhouse in the Yorkshire Dales, built in the 1840s. Three fields: the bottom field, the top field, and the long field along the beck. Dry stone walls. October. The light goes at four. Rain most days.

The house has been in the Moorhouse family since Henry's father. Henry never left it, not really. It is not the kind of house you visit. It is the kind of house you are from.

## Atmosphere

The house smells of decades. Stone, woodsmoke, lanolin, pipe tobacco. Not unpleasant — old. The smell of things worn and used.

October mornings in the Dales: fog in the bottom of the valley, the sheep appearing out of it. The beck audible but invisible. Cold that doesn't apologise.

## What Makes This World Particular

The farm doesn't know Henry is gone. The sheep are in the field. The gate needs its bolt checked on the second-to-last post. The range needs lighting at five-thirty. These things continue.
