## Overview

The farm sits in a fold in the hills above the valley. You would miss the lane if you didn't know to turn. Stone walls the colour of the hills. A farmhouse that has not been painted in twenty years and doesn't need to be.

October in the Dales is the end of something. The light is already going by three. The fog settles early in the bottom fields and stays. The sheep know the fields by smell and habit, the way Bess knows the house.

## What the Farm Holds

The farm holds forty years of Henry — in the walls, in the tools hung in the barn, in the particular angle of the gate to the top field that he fixed fifteen years ago and never needed to fix again. The farm holds Margaret too, though she's been gone eight years: in the sitting room boxes, in the garden Henry hasn't touched since.

It holds Bess.

## Atmosphere

The farm smells of itself: lanolin and stone, woodsmoke and cold mud. In the mornings, before the range is lit, the kitchen is the coldest room. After five-thirty — after Henry lights it — it becomes the warmest.

After Henry, someone has to decide what the kitchen becomes.

## The Land

The fields have names. Henry used them; Tom uses them; Bess knows them by the shape of the ground and the angle of the light. Anna doesn't know them. She will learn two of them before she leaves. That will be enough.
