# Chapter 002 — Summary · v3 (~250 words)

*For use as context when writing later chapters.*

---

Two weeks after Henry's death. Anna arrives in the afternoon, comes in through the front gate. Bess encounters her as a smell-map: the layers of her (city, unfamiliar soap, cold coffee, and something underneath that is not entirely foreign).

The chapter is structured not chronologically but sensorially — each short section anchored to a specific observation. Present tense throughout.

The house's existing smell-map (Tom's smell = reliability; the flat cap = Henry, thinning; the range = Tom's care) is established first. Anna's arrival is framed as a new smell entering and beginning to compete with the existing map.

Anna goes through the house; Bess stays in the hall. Anna sits on the floor at the end of the day, not knowing what else to do. Bess eats from the bowl she fills. Returns to the doorway. Neither moves for a long time.

**Variant character:** Present tense, impressionistic, shortest of the three. The chapter feels more like a poem or series of observations than a scene. Strongest sensory immersion — the most radical PoV alignment with Bess's actual mode of perception. Appropriate if the author wants to push further into non-human perspective. May be too fragmentary if the reader needs more grounding in human drama.
