# Chapter 002 — "New Smell" · v3

*Bold experiment. Present tense. Compressed. Structured around the smell of Anna as an intrusion into the house's existing smell-map. The chapter is shorter and more impressionistic — each section anchored to a specific sensory observation.*

---

The house has a smell now that it didn't have before.

Tom's smell she has learned: lanolin, engine oil, the outside, the particular farmyard that means he's come in through the yard gate. His smell is familiar enough. It means the bowl gets filled. It means someone has come, and will go, and will come back tomorrow.

The flat cap on the hook smells of Henry. Less than it did. She goes to it in the afternoons, stands under it, takes what is there.

Today there is a new smell.

---

The car is in the lane before it's in the yard — she hears the engine a few minutes before the gate. She goes to the kitchen doorway and stands. Tom came this morning and went. She is alone. The range is lit. This engine is not Tom's.

It comes into the yard and stops.

The front gate opens. This is wrong. Everyone comes round the back.

She stands in the kitchen doorway and waits.

---

The woman smells of a city. Coffee and diesel and a soap that is not the soap from the shelf above the sink. She comes in through the front door and stops in the hall and looks down at Bess.

"Bess," she says.

Not a call. A fact she is saying aloud.

Bess holds still. She is building a map of her — the layers of the smell, what is underneath the coat, what is underneath that. There is something underneath that is not entirely foreign. She cannot place it.

She lets the woman look at her. Then she steps back into the kitchen.

---

The woman goes through the house all afternoon. Shoes on the stairs — wrong shoes for the stairs, the sound of someone being careful. Doors. Drawers. She is making the house unfamiliar by moving through it.

Bess stays in the hall. She knows the rooms. She doesn't follow.

---

At some point the smell changes. The woman comes back to the kitchen and opens the pantry. She gets it wrong for a moment — reaching for the wrong shelf — then she reaches for one of the tins and Bess stands up.

The sound of the tin opener is the same. The same drawer, once she finds it. The same sound of the tin being turned into the bowl. The bowl placed on the stone floor.

The woman stands. Then she sits. Just sits on the kitchen floor with her back against the cupboards, her legs out, looking at the wall.

Bess eats.

The flat cap is on the hook by the door. The range has cooled since this morning. Outside it is getting dark.

No one goes anywhere.
