# Chapter 002 — Summary · v2 (~280 words)

*For use as context when writing later chapters.*

---

Two weeks after Henry's death. The same events as v1 but structured differently: the chapter opens with Tom's departure — his comment "She'll be here this afternoon" addressed to no one in particular — and then Bess is alone until Anna arrives.

Tom washes his hands at the yard tap, checks the gates, gives Bess a brief look, and walks down the lane. Bess watches him go.

Anna arrives at two in the afternoon. She drives the lane slowly and comes in through the front gate, front door. She smells of city. Bess and Anna regard each other. Anna says her name like a reminder.

The chapter follows Anna's afternoon in the house: shoes on the stairs, doors, drawers. Anna learning the space the hard way. Bess stays in the hall throughout.

At five Anna returns to the kitchen and opens the wrong cupboards. Bess goes to the pantry door. Anna follows the cue, finds the tins, locates the tin opener (in the drawer by the sink, after looking in two others). Opens the tin, fills the bowl, places it on the floor.

Then she sits on the floor with her back against the cupboards. Looks at the far wall.

Bess eats. Returns to the kitchen doorway. Anna still there. Flat cap on the hook. Range cooling toward evening. No one speaks.

**Variant character:** Opens at the moment of transition (Tom leaving). More compressed — skips the morning scene. Stronger contrast between Tom's ease in the space and Anna's foreignness. Cooler emotional register. Ends the same as v1 but with slightly more distance.
