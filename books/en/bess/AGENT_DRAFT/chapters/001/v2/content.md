# Chapter 001 — "The Morning He Didn't Come Down" / Variant v2

---

Tom let himself in through the back at seven-fifteen, the way he had every morning for twenty-two years.

He called "Henry?" once. It wasn't a question. It was more like the sound you make when you arrive somewhere — announcing yourself to the house.

Then he saw Bess at the top of the stairs.

He was very still for a moment. He set his keys down on the kitchen table. Then he went up.

---

She had woken before five, the way she always did. It was still dark.

The house smelled right — pipe tobacco, the cold stone of the hall, the just-fading warmth of the range. She went to the foot of the stairs and sat down. She waited.

Five-thirty: nothing.

Six: nothing.

The sky outside turned grey, then pale. She could hear the sheep beginning to move in the bottom field. She could hear the beck over the stone. She could not hear the creak of the bedroom floor, the sound of his knees on the third step.

She went up.

The bedroom door was ajar. She pushed it open with her nose and went in.

He was very still. She went to him and touched his hand and his hand was cold. She lay down beside the bed on the floor and waited. She did not know what she was waiting for. Waiting was what she did.

---

After Tom, there were others. She heard the cars in the yard — engines she didn't know, footsteps she didn't know, voices. She went to the kitchen. She lay under his cap.

Someone eventually came and filled her bowl.

She ate. Then she went back to the foot of the stairs.

She waited there until dark.
