# Chapter 002 — Summary ✦ APPROVED (~280 words)

*For use as context when writing later chapters.*

---

Two weeks after Henry's death. Tom has maintained the farm routine — lighting the range each morning, feeding Bess, tending the sheep — but he comes later and says less.

That morning Tom returns from the top field and goes home. The farm is quiet.

In the afternoon a car comes down the lane slowly and stops in the yard. A woman — Anna, Henry's daughter, arriving from Leeds to deal with the estate — comes in through the front gate and the front door. She smells of wool, city, diesel, unfamiliar soap, cold coffee.

Bess and Anna look at each other from opposite ends of the hall. Anna says Bess's name as though reminding herself of it.

Tom returns briefly. He and Anna speak in the kitchen — careful voices. Then he leaves.

Anna goes through the house slowly, opening doors, standing in them. She looks at the flat cap on the hook for a long time. She tries the wrong cupboards looking for dog food.

Bess watches from doorways throughout.

At some point Anna finds the pantry, selects a tin, locates the tin opener after trying two drawers, and opens it into Bess's bowl. She puts the bowl on the floor.

Bess doesn't move.

Anna sits down on the kitchen floor with her back against the cupboards and looks at the far wall.

Bess crosses the kitchen and eats. She returns to the doorway. Anna is still on the floor.

The flat cap is on its hook.

Neither of them moves for a long time.
