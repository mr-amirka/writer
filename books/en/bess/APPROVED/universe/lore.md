## Realism Without Fantasy

The world of this book is realistic. No magic, no speculative premise.
The only "shift" — the narrative runs through a dog's perception.

## Core Rule: Canine Epistemology

Bess sees and feels everything — but interprets differently:
- Death — "the smell changed and he doesn't move"
- Love — "she is warm and she opens tins"
- Grief — "she doesn't understand why everything is wrong"

Dogs in this book **do not speak, do not think in words, do not "understand humans."** They perceive the world sensorially. The narrator translates this into language — but keeps dog logic throughout.

## What Bess Can Do

- Read a smell more precisely than any person
- Feel a person's mood through how they breathe and move
- Wait — indefinitely, without frustration
- Stay — not out of sympathy, just because

## What Bess Cannot Do

- Understand "why"
- Understand "tomorrow" or "never"
- Understand money, inheritance, death, or loss
- Choose between staying and going — she simply stays
