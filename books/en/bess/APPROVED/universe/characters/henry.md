## Presence (only through traces)

The reader never sees him alive. Only through what remains:
- The smell of pipe tobacco and lanolin (fading)
- His flat cap on the hook by the kitchen door
- The tools in the barn, arranged the way he arranged them
- The gate to the top field, latched the way he always latched it
- Tom, who still comes every morning

## Canonical Facts

- Lived at the farm since he was born; never left Yorkshire except once (honeymoon, Scarborough, 1978)
- Margaret, his wife, died eight years ago
- Daughter Anna: left at seventeen, rarely came back
- Raised Bess from eight weeks old (spring 2014)
- Talked to Bess — practical, brief: "Come by." "Away." "Good lass."
- Died in the night, in his sleep, heart

## Presence in the Book

Henry is only present through consequences. But he must be very real — through details, smells, objects, the patterns he left in the farm. Every appearance of his trace should carry weight.
