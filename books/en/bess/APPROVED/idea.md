# Approved Idea — The Last Field

A border collie named Bess has lived ten years on a Yorkshire farm. When her owner dies one morning, Bess stays. She waits at the foot of the stairs where she has always waited. She doesn't know what death is. She doesn't know what "never" means. A daughter arrives from the city to close the estate — and finds she can't close everything.

The gap between what the dog perceives and what the reader understands is where the book lives.
