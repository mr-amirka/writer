## Genre References and Author Influences

- Tone: Penelope Fitzgerald's restraint — nothing said that doesn't need saying.
- Atmosphere analogue: *The Gate of Angels*, *The Bookshop*. Also Elizabeth Taylor's rural England.
- Bess as unreliable narrator — her incomprehension is not comic; it's sad.

## What the Author Values in This Book

- Contrast: the ordinary detail carries all the emotional weight (the cap, the bucket, the gate)
- Bess must not be "humanised" — she stays a dog with dog logic
- Each chapter ends on a concrete image, not an emotional conclusion
