# Chapter 001 — Author's Draft

*Raw. For agent: interpret into three variants.*

---

Before five. That's when Bess always wakes up. Before Henry comes down.

She's at the foot of the stairs. She's been at the foot of the stairs for ten years. This is where she waits.

The house smells of Henry: pipe tobacco, lanolin, old wax jacket hanging in the hall. Also cold stone and woodsmoke from last night's range.

She waits. The light changes in the kitchen — she can see it under the door. Pale. Grey. The light that means it's getting on for five-thirty.

Henry comes down at five-thirty.

He doesn't come down.

She waits. The light changes again. The kitchen goes from grey to yellow. That means the sun. That means it's past seven. Henry is never in bed past six.

Something is wrong. She knows this through her nose before she knows it any other way. The smell of Henry is there — everywhere in the house — but it's cold. Not warm. Wrong kind of Henry smell.

She goes upstairs. She almost never goes upstairs. The carpet on the stairs. The sound of the bedroom door.

She goes in.

Henry is in the bed. She goes to him. Touches his hand with her nose. Cold.

She stays.

---

Tom comes at seven. He lets himself in through the kitchen, the way he always does. Calls "Henry?" once, the way he always does.

Then he sees Bess at the top of the stairs.

He doesn't call again.
