# Style: Main — Author's Notes

*Raw. For agent: interpret, don't edit.*

---

Short sentences. Simple words. Yorkshire taciturnity.

The narrator records. Doesn't comment. Doesn't reassure.

Emotion is in the white space. Between "the door didn't open" and the next paragraph.

No: "She felt a wave of grief." Yes: "The door didn't open. Bess waited until dark."

Physical details carry everything. Cap on the hook. Bucket by the door. The smell of something that was warm.

Dialogue: almost none. When it comes, it's short. What's said matters; what isn't said matters more.

References: Penelope Fitzgerald, Elizabeth Taylor, Marilynne Robinson. Plain English. Nothing decorative.
