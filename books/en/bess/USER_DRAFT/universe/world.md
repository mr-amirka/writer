# World — Author's Notes

*Raw. For agent: interpret, don't edit.*

---

Yorkshire Dales. Not the pretty tourist version. The working version — mud and stone walls and the sound of wind through gaps in the barn door.

The farmhouse is small. Two rooms downstairs, two up. Kitchen, sitting room. The kitchen is the real room — range, table, two chairs, Bess's blanket in the corner. The sitting room is where things go when there's nowhere else for them. Three boxes Henry never unpacked after Margaret died. A shelf of farming almanacs. A chair that's too good to use.

Outside: three fields. Top field, bottom field, the long field along the beck. Dry stone walls in between. The walls are older than the farmhouse.

The sheep: Dalesbred, mostly. Henry knew each one. Anna doesn't know any of them. Tom knows all of them.

October. Rain every other day. The light goes at four o'clock.

---

The farm has been in the family since Henry's father. Henry never talked about selling it. He also never talked about not selling it. He just farmed.

Margaret died eight years ago. After that Henry kept on. Tom helped. Bess helped. The neighbours sometimes helped with lambing.

Anna grew up here until she was seventeen, then went to university and didn't come back much. Christmas sometimes. Easter once. She married, divorced, kept the flat in Leeds.

She didn't expect to inherit this. She thought there'd be more time.

---

Distances and neighbours:
- Village: 3 miles. Post office, pub, church.
- Tom's house: half a mile down the lane.
- The nearest town: 8 miles. Supermarket, solicitors.
- Leeds (where Anna lives): 1 hour 20 minutes by car.
