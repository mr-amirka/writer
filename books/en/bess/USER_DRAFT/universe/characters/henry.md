# Henry — Author's Notes

*Raw character sketch. He's dead before the book starts.*

---

Henry Moorhouse. 72. Farmer. Dalesbred sheep, three fields, stone farmhouse.

He never left Yorkshire except once, for his honeymoon (Scarborough, a week, 1978). He didn't see the point of going far.

He was quiet. Not unhappy — quiet. Said things once. If you didn't catch it that was your problem.

He loved the farm the way you love something you've never questioned. It wasn't a choice. He just was there.

Margaret, his wife, died eight years ago. He didn't talk about it. He kept farming. That was the answer, if he had one.

Anna is his daughter. They weren't close, exactly. But they were his, so he loved them. He would have done anything for her if she'd asked. She didn't ask.

Bess: he raised her from eight weeks. He talked to her. Not sentiment — practical. "Come by." "Away." "Good lass."

He left no instructions for what to do after.
