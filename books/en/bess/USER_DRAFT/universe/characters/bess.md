# Bess — Author's Notes

*Raw character sketch.*

---

Border collie. Ten years old. Mostly black with white front and white tip to tail.

She's been working with Henry since she was eight weeks old. Sheep. The farm. He trained her — not the way you'd explain to someone how she was trained, but the way it happens, over years, until she just knows.

She reads him. When he's tired. When the weather's coming. When something's wrong with a sheep. She's better at Henry than Henry is.

She sleeps at the foot of the stairs. Every night. She's always been there when he came down.

---

Her world is:
- Smell first (who's there, what's changed, what's wrong)
- Sound second (footsteps, gate, voice)
- The farm — she knows every inch of it by smell

She doesn't know what death is. She knows Henry doesn't come down. She waits.

She's not sentimental. She's precise. She notices.

That's the point of her.
