# World Rules — Author's Notes

*Raw. For agent: interpret, don't edit.*

---

No magic. The real world. The only "shift" is that the narrative runs through a dog's perception.

Dog epistemology: Bess perceives through smell, sound, temperature, and routine. She does not have concepts for death, ownership, money, grief, or "never." She has:
- Smell (who is here, how they feel, what is different)
- Sound (footsteps on stairs, the gate latch, the range clicking)
- Temperature (warm = safe, cold = wrong)
- Routine (morning = this happens, then this, then this; if it doesn't — wait)

When something is absent she waits. She doesn't conclude "gone." She concludes "not yet."

Dogs in this book do not speak, do not think in words, do not "understand people." The narrator translates Bess's experience into language — but keeps dog logic throughout.

---

What Bess can do:
- Read a person's mood through how they move and breathe
- Know who has been in a room from the smell they left
- Wait — indefinitely, without frustration
- Be present — not out of sympathy, just because

What Bess cannot do:
- Understand "why"
- Understand "tomorrow" or "never"
- Understand money, inheritance, or loss
- Choose between staying and going — she simply stays
